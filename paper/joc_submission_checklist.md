## Fit Assessment

Journal of Classification is a plausible next target as a short note. The
paper is about classification methodology, tree-structured models, statistical
learning, and computational classification. It is a better conceptual fit than
a broad pattern-recognition venue if the contribution is framed as a compact
geometric extension to recursive partitioning rather than as a large benchmark
paper.

Residual risk: the journal may expect deeper statistical methodology than the
current prototype note provides. The submission should therefore be positioned
modestly as a short note introducing a split family and documenting its main
properties, not as a definitive new algorithm.

## Guideline Checks

- Article type: prepared as a short note.
- Peer review: manuscript is blinded; author details are only on the title
  page and cover letter.
- Layout: manuscript is single-column, 12 pt, double-spaced, with wide margins.
- Abstract: no more than 150 words.
- Length: manuscript is below the 50-page maximum.
- Data matrices: the data matrix `X` and response vector `y` are defined near
  the beginning of the manuscript.
- Vectors/matrices: vectors and matrices are marked in bold mathematical font.
- Figures/tables: included within the 4.8 in by 6.8 in maximum display size.
- Data availability: statement included before references.
- Code availability: statement included after data availability and before
  references.
- Supplementary code: reproduction instructions are prepared in blinded form.
